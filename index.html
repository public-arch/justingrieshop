— 01 · Research

Foundations that hold.

An independent program in finite, periodic arithmetic. The thesis: the integers carry more native structure than mainstream mathematics treats them as carrying. The continuum is a derived object built over a finite substrate. Most barriers in modern math are artifacts of method — not properties of the underlying objects.

The program is called Marithmetics. It has its own canonical site at marithmetics.com, where every claim is paired with a deterministic demonstration, a published paper, and a public attack surface.

The work is conservative on purpose. Every claim has to compress to a finite, checkable object before it leaves the workshop. Every demo is reproducible. Every paper has a published method of falsification. Audit-grade, applied to mathematics instead of accounting.

— About

A servant operator.

I lead AI & Analytics for a large enterprise, run an independent research program, and wrote a book on what Plato called the missing substrate. The through-line is the same in every domain: strip the noise, build from the foundation, amplify the humans doing the work. The future belongs to those who build it.

The work looks scattered until you see the method. In the enterprise, that means leading transformation across nine functional domains — data engineering, applied AI, architecture, change management — with the discipline of an operator and the bearing of a servant leader. In research, it means treating mathematical objects as exact finite sets rather than statistical approximations, and pushing every claim to closure or rejection rather than to summary. In writing, it means showing rather than arguing — six or seven pages at a time, fifteen minutes per book, the work of many years.

I believe in signal as a strategic asset. In an era of noise, the operators who learn to extract it compound. The ones who can't, don't. AI is the single highest-leverage instrument for that extraction ever built — provided you use it on humans, not against them.

I live near Cincinnati. I read carefully. I think slowly on purpose.
